FAQ

What is Mediacorp’s role in Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games?

As Singapore’s Olympics Network, Mediacorp is the official media network for the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games (24 Jul – 9 Aug) and will deliver the widest coverage of the world’s largest sporting event across its multiple platforms including Mediacorp’s over-the-top video service, meWATCH, Channel 5 and YouTube.

meWATCH will carry coverage of the sporting action from Tokyo 2020 LIVE and on-demand across 14 dedicated channels.

The Opening and Closing Ceremonies will be shown LIVE on meWATCH and Channel 5.

What sports will be included in Mediacorp’s coverage of the Tokyo 2020?

The following are the sports that will be shown either LIVE or delayed in our daily coverage: Swimming, Diving, Badminton, Table Tennis, Athletics, Football, Taekwondo, Basketball, Boxing, Gymnastics and Volleyball, subject to availability of the coverage.

For more details, visit mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020

Where can I catch all the action?

For the most comprehensive coverage of Tokyo 2020, look no further than meWATCH starting with the Opening Ceremony on 24 Jul. Mediacorp will have LIVE coverage across 14 channels on meWATCH dedicated to Tokyo 2020.

Users of the meWATCH app and meWATCH site mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020 can expect a specially curated 24-hour Olympic Mediacorp Channel and Olympic News Channel that brings round-the-clock updates on the latest from the Olympic Games.

On top of that, you can also get 12 channels of LIVE/near-LIVE wall-to wall programming daily across 14 hours each day, plus Video-on-demand (VOD) of the highlights, thrills & spills, record breaking feats, Team Singapore winning moments and highlights.

For more details, visit mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020. You may access the coverage of Tokyo 2020 anytime, anywhere, LIVE or on-demand, via the free meWATCH app, available on iOS and Android devices, Smart TVs, or on your computer.

You will need to sign in to meWATCH to watch Tokyo 2020 for free. All users may sign in to meWATCH via your meCONNECT or Social account. meCONNECT is Mediacorp’s single sign-on platform to access our digital services. If you do not have an account, create one for free at mewatch.sg/signup.

Channel 5 will broadcast the Opening Ceremony on 24 July from 7pm to 10pm and the Closing Ceremony 9 August from 9.30pm to 12.30am. You can also catch the daily action from Tokyo 2020 from 25 July to 8 August at these times on Channel 5:

  • ● 9am to 12pm
  • ● 3pm to 8.30pm
  • ● 10pm to 2am

Mediacorp will also produce a daily round-up show “Today At The Games” featuring Team Singapore’s performance at Tokyo 2020 as well as highlights from the Games. The programme will be available on meWATCH and also on Channel 5 daily from 25 July to 8 Aug at 9.30pm.

Viewers can also expect daily news updates on CNA news bulletins and across all of Mediacorp’s news bulletins.

Why must I sign in to meWATCH to enjoy the Tokyo 2020 coverage when it is free?

This is part of our on-going efforts to improve our services so that we are able to understand our users better, and provide a more targeted and personalised experience for you.

Why do I only need to sign in to meWATCH watch Tokyo 2020, and not other free content?

The coverage of Tokyo 2020, along with the 30th SEA Games in 2019 and the Winter Youth Olympic Games earlier this year, are a few of the free LIVE events that we have implemented mandatory sign-in. This is part of our efforts to improve our services, and provide a more targeted and personalised experience for you.

If I don’t want to sign in, can I still watch the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games?

You can watch a curated coverage of Tokyo 2020 on Channel 5, as well as highlights of the sporting events on meWATCH’s YouTube channel at youtube.com/meWATCH.

Why doesn’t Channel 5’s simulcast on meWATCH require me to sign in?

Channel 5 is not showing the full coverage of Tokyo 2020. Sign-in is only required to view the full coverage on meWATCH, which gives you access to 14 dedicated channels.

What Games content are available on Channel 5 and meWATCH’s YouTube channel?

Mediacorp’s Tokyo 2020 coverage on Channel 5 and meWATCH’s YouTube includes:

  • 1. Opening Ceremony Live (Live), 24 Jul, 7pm
  • 2. Daily Action Belt (Live/near-live): 25 Jul - 8 Aug (9am – 2am)
  • 3. Today At The Games (Daily Highlights), 25 Jul – 8 Aug, 9.30pm
  • 4. Summary of the Games, 9 Aug, 5pm
  • 5. Closing Ceremony Live (Delayed), 9 Aug, 9.30pm
How do I sign in?

The sign-in process is convenient and simple. You may do so using your meCONNECT or Social account.

Where do I sign in from?

You can sign in via the meWATCH app on your mobile device, tablet and compatible TV device or at Mediacorp’s official Tokyo 2020 microsite mediacorp.sg/Tokyo2020. Alternatively, you may also access the meWATCH website through your mobile and computer/laptop web browser and sign in from there.

What are the benefits of signing in?

Enjoy the following benefits with meWATCH Sign-in:

  • ● My Watchlist: You can create your watchlist to access your favourite programmes quickly, and get updated on new episodes.
  • ● Pause & Resume watching: Pick up from where you left off, on multiple devices.
  • ● Download and Watch Offline: Download your favourite programmes on Wi-Fi and watch on the go without data charges.
If I miss any of the sports events LIVE on meWATCH, where can I watch them?

Video-on-demand coverage of Tokyo 2020 will be available on the free meWATCH app for both iOS and Android devices, Smart TVs, or on mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020

Will Channel 5/meWATCH showcase all Team Singapore sporting events?

Mediacorp will provide an extensive coverage of the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games, with a focus on Team Singapore events where possible. Given the fluid nature of the competition schedule, there may be last minute programme changes. For more details, please visit mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020 or our social media sites for the latest updates.

Where can I check the game schedule and what’s available for viewing?

The schedule of the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games coverage is available at mewatch.sg/Tokyo2020. The “Sports Finder” function allows users to zoom in to their favourite sports quickly. You can also add upcoming games to your calendars and be reminded prior to the start of the event.

Where do I go to for more information about Mediacorp’s coverage of the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games?

For more information on how you can get the most comprehensive coverage of the Games, please visit mediacorp.sg/Tokyo2020.

If you are experiencing technical problems accessing the games on meWATCH, please click on “Report a problem” tool on meWATCH. Should you have any further queries or would like to provide feedback, please write to enquiry@mewatch.sg.
